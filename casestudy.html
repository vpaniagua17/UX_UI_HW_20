Stand up
for Kids

My team was granted the task of redesigning an existing non-profits website. We chose to stay true to some of the existing parent design elements & components and modernize and reorganize the desktop and mobile site in order to create an easier user-experience.

User Research

My team found that the intial site was note easy to navigate. Our team had one goal in mind, we wanted to make the process of donating as simple for the user as possible as well as providing necessary information in a clear form.

Ideation Research

After our first stakeholder interview with the direct of communication at Stand up for Kids, we established that the sites main users are donors. We wanted to make the user experience to donate as straightforward and simple as possible. In this research project, we planned and conducted 5 interviews with 1 stakeholder, current users and potential users in San Francisco, CA.

Usability Testing

After several itterations and implementing user feedback we settled on a design that we felt still invoked the essenace of the original site.

Our style guide was our north star for this redesign. We decided to keep the original purple color component from the original website but instead of keeping it monochromatic we implemented secondary colors. We chose a pastel color palette which invokes a calming user experience. We ran several user testings and made adjustments as we began to redesign and alter our original mockups.

Prototyping

After several iterations we finally settled on a prototyle that we felt was simple to navigate and created a simple user flowe for donating.
